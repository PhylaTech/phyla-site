# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Phyla's site has to convert four overlapping but distinct visitor types in a single session:

- **Biotech founders, CEOs, CSOs.** Non-engineer scientific leaders evaluating consultants for a discovery or platform problem. Will read prose; will not read jargon. Decide on "these people get it."
- **Heads of Platform / VPs of Engineering at biotech.** Technical buyer. Judges depth in seconds: what's the stack, what have you shipped, can you operate at our scale.
- **ML / data scientists at biotech.** Practitioner-level. Want real chops: papers, code, repos. Will close the tab on the first cliché.
- **Investors and peer referrals.** Land here to vet Phyla before making an introduction. Need a fast trust signal.

The site has to work for all four in a single visit. The most senior visitors are not the most technical; the most technical are not the most patient with marketing voice.

## Product Purpose

Phyla Technologies is a small, founder-led consultancy that builds and operates ML and data infrastructure for life sciences companies: genomics pipelines, model deployment, bio/cheminformatics, dashboards, AI/LLM integration. Three scientists and engineers (two PhDs, one CS-trained) with shipped work across drug discovery, genomics, and production ML.

**Heritage.** The team's research roots are in **natural products** and **pharmacognosy**, the study of medicines from plants, fungi, and microbes. The name "Phyla" is the plural of *phylum*, the Linnaean rank that organizes life into kingdoms and clades. This lineage is not decoration. It shapes what Phyla actually does (the team came up through molecular property prediction, metagenomics, computational drug discovery) and it shapes how the brand should feel: rooted in the long history of cataloguing the natural world, not in the recent gold rush of AI startups.

The site exists to **generate qualified inbound**: biotech leaders fill out the contact form because they have a real engagement to discuss, not because they want a pitch. The Impact page (live OpenAlex citation data, 76 countries and 22 disciplines) is the proof-of-work that justifies the inbound.

Success looks like: a head of platform at a Series B biotech reads the site, pre-qualifies Phyla against bigger consultancies and solo shops, and books a call already convinced the team is real.

## Brand Personality

**Rigorous, warm, ambitious, and rooted.** Four words because the heritage matters:

- **Rigorous.** Quiet expertise. The PhDs and the citation data do the talking.
- **Warm.** Three humans who chose this work because they love it. Not a logo wall, not a faceless agency.
- **Ambitious.** Operating at the frontier of what biotech companies can build. Not a dev shop, not a body shop, not a generic data team.
- **Rooted.** Connected to the long lineage of natural-products science and biological classification. Modern technology in service of an old, careful tradition.

Voice: confident without being loud, technical without being exclusionary, human without being cute. No buzzword soup, no restated headings, no em dashes.

## Anti-references

Phyla should not look like any of these. The site explicitly rejects each one's reflex:

- **Generic SaaS landing.** No Stripe-clone hero with gradient blob, no logo wall, no big-number stat template, no identical card grids of icon + title + two lines of copy.
- **Big-consultancy corporate (Deloitte / BCG).** No stock photos, no "global solutions" copy, no navy-and-grey palette, no business-speak paragraphs.
- **Crypto / Web3 / AI-startup neon.** No neon-on-black, no glowing borders as signature, no animated mesh gradient as identity, no glassmorphism as default surface, no teal-cyan-blue-violet rainbow gradient. **The current site leans heavily this way and is the explicit wrong direction.** It was AI-generated boilerplate; the redesign moves decisively away from it.
- **Academic / lab homepage.** No sidebar of publications in serif, no 1998 university CSS, nothing that reads as unfunded or pre-professional. ("Rooted in natural-history tradition" does not mean "looks like a 2003 university faculty page.")

If a design choice could plausibly belong in any of those four, replace it.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the receipts.** Phyla's edge is real scientific output. Surface the citations, the publications, the named team. Proof beats claims, every time.
2. **Practice what we preach.** A team selling MLOps and bespoke software cannot ship a site that feels generic, broken, or templated. The interface is part of the credibility argument.
3. **Speak to four readers in one voice.** Every section must feel right to a non-engineer CSO, a senior platform engineer, a working ML scientist, and a VC scanning in 30 seconds. No element optimized for one audience should alienate another.
4. **Quiet confidence over volume.** No exclamation marks, no maximalist hype. The work is the loud part; the layout does not have to be.
5. **Reject the first reflex of the category.** Biotech, MLOps, AI-tools, and consultancy each have a visual cliché. Phyla's site lives in the gaps between them.
6. **Honor the natural-products lineage.** The brand draws on a centuries-old tradition of cataloguing, classifying, and learning from the living world. Phyla's identity should feel closer to a modern field guide, a working herbarium, or a contemporary natural-history press than to a tech-startup landing page. Earth-toned, taxonomic, careful: modern in execution, ancient in lineage.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Floor: WCAG 2.2 AA on contrast, keyboard navigation, alt text, `prefers-reduced-motion` respected. Above the floor, optimize for visual quality. If a design tradeoff arises, prefer the more accessible solution unless it visibly weakens the design.
